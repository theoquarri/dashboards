Key Findings & Recommended Actions

  1. Vertically integrated model is a competitive moat — but the CFO challenge is understanding margin contribution across channels. Revenue flows through DTC, wholesale (Pacsun, Zumiez, Winners, Nordstrom, Scheels, Buckle, Boathouse), licensed IP (Universal, Lucasfilm, UFC, Live Nation, Tim Hortons), and university/corporate programmes. A unified view of profitability by channel, brand, and product is the critical gap.
  2. DTC is the highest-margin channel but requires disciplined drop execution. Conversion rate, AOV, CAC, and repeat purchase rate determine whether DTC is a profit engine or a cost centre. Precise inventory planning per drop is essential to avoid stockouts and markdown risk.
  3. Wholesale is predictable but margin-compressed — which partners generate the best return on complexity? Sell-through rate by partner is the key metric. Partners with high sell-through and fast payment justify the operational overhead; those with high markdowns and slow payment do not.
  4. Licensed IP drives demand but carries operational risk. Each licensor (Universal, Lucasfilm, UFC, Live Nation) has strict approval processes, compliance requirements, and royalty structures. Late approvals delay production; compliance failures risk licence revocation.
  5. In-house production from Toronto/Buffalo is a supply chain advantage — but capacity utilisation drives unit economics. Screen-printing, embroidery, sewing, digital print, and finishing (MOQ 144 units) enable fast turnaround. Production lines must run at 70%+ utilisation to achieve target unit costs.

Licensing & IP Portfolio

Production, Operations & Cost Structure
In-house production from Toronto (manufacturing) and Buffalo (US distribution) handles screen-printing, embroidery, sewing, digital print, finishing, and packing at MOQ 144 units.
